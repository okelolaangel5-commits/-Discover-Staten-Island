Fun Facts

The Staten Island Ferry runs 24/7 and is completely free.
It is the greenest borough with parks, beaches, and wetlands.
Freshkills Park was once the world’s largest landfill.
You get one of the best NYC skyline views from Staten Island.

History

Staten Island was originally home to the Lenape Native Americans. In the 1600s, it was settled by the Dutch and later taken over by the British. In 1898, Staten Island officially became one of the five boroughs of New York City.

Timeline

1609: First European exploration.

1661: First permanent settlement.

1898: Staten Island joins NYC.

1964: Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge opens.

2001: Freshkills landfill closes.

Politics & Government

Staten Island is governed as part of New York City and has a Borough President. It is politically distinct, often voting more conservatively than other boroughs. Residents are represented in the NYC Council and New York State government.
